Why Clean Earth?

Air pollution levels have been rising steadily, impacting health and the environment.

Global Air Quality Index (AQI):

Over 90% of the world's population lives in areas where air quality exceeds WHO guidelines.

Health Impact:

Air pollution is responsible for approximately 7 million premature deaths annually.

Economic Cost:

The economic cost of air pollution is estimated to be over $5 trillion each year in welfare losses.

Major Pollutants:

Common pollutants include PM2.5, nitrogen dioxide (NO2), and sulfur dioxide (SO2).

    Welcome to Clean Earth

    The Clean Earth campaign is dedicated to combating the growing threat of air pollution and ensuring a healthier, more sustainable future for all. By raising awareness, empowering communities, and advocating for policy changes, the campaign seeks to drastically reduce air pollution levels globally and protect the environment for future generations.

    Our Work

    Since the launch of the Clean Earth campaign, we have successfully planted over 10,000 trees in urban areas, significantly improving local air quality.

    Our workshops have reached over 5,000 families, educating them on the importance of clean air and how to protect their children.

    Collaborations with local governments have resulted in the implementation of new policies that have reduced emissions by 15% in targeted areas.

    Community Workshops: We organized workshops in schools and community centers to educate children and parents about the dangers of air pollution and how to reduce exposure.

    Tree Planting Initiatives: We initiated tree planting drives in urban areas to improve air quality, as trees absorb CO2 and release oxygen, creating a healthier environment for children

    Promote Sustainable Practices: Our campaigns encourage individuals and communities to adopt sustainable practices that reduce air pollution. This includes promoting the use of public transportation, carpooling, cycling, and walking, as well as advocating for the use of clean energy sources.

    Collaboration with Local Governments: Partnered with local authorities to implement stricter regulations on emissions from factories and vehicles, ensuring cleaner air for our communities.

    Through awareness and action, we aim to reduce air pollution levels and promote clean air initiatives.
